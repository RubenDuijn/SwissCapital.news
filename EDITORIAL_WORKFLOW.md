# SwissCapital.news Editorial Workflow

This workflow turns the editorial doctrine into daily execution.

## Purpose
Ensure every published piece reflects SwissCapital.news identity:
- institutional
- analytical
- restrained
- systems-oriented
- high-signal

## Workflow Stages
1. Idea Intake
2. Thesis Framing
3. Research and Evidence
4. Drafting
5. Internal Editorial Review
6. Final Quality Gate
7. Publication and Distribution
8. Post-Publication Review

## Stage 1: Idea Intake
Goal:
- decide whether an idea belongs to SwissCapital.news

Required input:
- one-sentence topic
- why now
- structural relevance

Pass criteria:
- topic intersects capital, technology, institutions, geopolitics, or infrastructure
- topic has long-cycle implications

Fail signals:
- pure headline reaction
- trend-chasing without structural relevance

Outcome:
- proceed or reject

## Stage 2: Thesis Framing
Goal:
- lock a precise argument before writing

Required output:
- opening thesis (1 to 3 sentences)
- structural claim
- preliminary capital implication

Pass criteria:
- thesis is specific and falsifiable
- article has a non-generic viewpoint

Fail signals:
- broad summary thesis
- no clear structural claim

Outcome:
- approved thesis statement

## Stage 3: Research and Evidence
Goal:
- collect evidence that supports structural and incentive analysis

Minimum evidence set:
- institutional sources (policy docs, filings, disclosures, official data)
- cross-domain context (technology + capital + policy)
- at least one historical or comparative anchor

Pass criteria:
- evidence supports each major claim
- sources are credible and current

Fail signals:
- narrative-first writing with thin evidence
- overreliance on secondary commentary

Outcome:
- source-backed outline

## Stage 4: Drafting
Goal:
- produce first full draft in SwissCapital structure

Required structure:
1. Opening thesis
2. Structural analysis
3. Incentive analysis
4. Capital implications
5. Long-term systemic interpretation

Pass criteria:
- clear section logic
- measured institutional tone
- no hype language

Fail signals:
- emotional framing
- startup-style copy
- daily-news cadence

Outcome:
- draft ready for review

## Stage 5: Internal Editorial Review
Goal:
- assess strategic coherence and publication fit

Review checklist:
- does this sound like SwissCapital.news
- does it add true signal
- are second-order effects explicit
- are uncertainties calibrated correctly

Pass criteria:
- reviewer can summarize unique insight in one sentence
- no identity drift

Fail signals:
- generic AI tone
- weak cross-domain synthesis
- unclear implications

Outcome:
- revise or proceed

## Stage 6: Final Quality Gate
Mandatory file:
- ARTICLE_ACCEPTANCE_CHECKLIST.md

Decision:
- Publish
- Revise
- Reject

Hard rule:
- if any identity gate fails, do not publish

## Stage 7: Publication and Distribution
Goal:
- publish with confidence and restrained framing

Publication standards:
- clean headline
- clear dek
- no urgency theater
- no clickbait

Distribution guidance:
- website is primary identity
- newsletter is secondary distribution infrastructure
- social posts should lead with thesis, not promotion

## Stage 8: Post-Publication Review
Timing:
- 7 to 14 days after publication

Review questions:
- did the piece remain useful beyond immediate headlines
- did it attract the right audience quality
- did the argument age well
- what to improve for next cycle

Outcome:
- refinement notes added to editorial planning

## Cadence Policy
- publish selectively
- prioritize foundational quality over frequency
- if uncertain, delay publication and strengthen analysis

Publication doctrine:
- one exceptional article is superior to ten generic pieces

## Operating Roles (can be one person)
- Editor: thesis quality and identity guard
- Analyst: evidence and structural depth
- Reviewer: final gate against hype and genericity

## Practical Sequence
1. Capture idea
2. Approve thesis
3. Build evidence map
4. Draft in five-part structure
5. Run editorial review
6. Run final acceptance checklist
7. Publish
8. Review performance and learning
